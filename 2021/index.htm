2021 NAB Show

The ultimate event for media, entertainment and technology professionals

THE UNRIVALED EVENT FOR CONTENT PROFESSIONALS IN MEDIA AND ENTERTAINMENT

    It’s All Coming Together.

    With AES Show, Radio Show, NAB SMTE and other co-located events yet to be announced, October’s NAB Show® will be the most significant, in-person media and entertainment event of 2021. The industry will realize unparalleled access to critical information, inspiration and solutions for those seeking to create superior audio and visual experiences that attract listeners and viewers in large numbers for greater reach.

    “The ability to meet with a large number of our current and potential customers is the reason we return to NAB Show year after year, and that opportunity was sorely missed in 2020. We look forward to once again meet with our customers face-to-face. There is truly no better way to learn about their goals and deliver solutions that meet their needs.” 

    Ken Frommert, President, Enco Systems, Inc 

    “We value the many opportunities that participating in NAB Show affords us, and we look forward to continuing our partnership. We’re excited to reconnect in-person with our customers at NAB Show in October, and we also look forward to meeting new ones.”

    Bob Canigila, Director of Sales Operations Americas, Blackmagic Design 

    “There are so many important changes happening right now in our industry.  It’s critical for our business that we take advantage of the opportunity to stay educated and on top of these issues. I am really looking forward to NAB Show, especially this year as we look to accelerate deployment of Nextgen TV.  I love discovering innovations from startups in Futures Park, finding new technology on the exhibit floor, and networking with colleagues and vendors.  I’m energized just thinking about the chance to see everyone face-to-face again.” 

    Brett Jenkins, CTO, Nextar Media Group  

    “The Alabama Broadcasters Association is thrilled to offer, for the first-time, scholarships to make it possible for 16 of our members to attend NAB Show in October. This is a way to make sure that, despite the difficult financial circumstances stations are experiencing, we are supporting and encouraging our members to attend this can’t miss event for broadcasters.”

    Sharon M. Tinsley, President, Alabama Broadcasters Association 

    So far, over 600 companies have reserved their space on the show floor. Those included in these early commitments represent more than 31 nations, including China, Germany, India, Israel and Uruguay. And major brands such as AT&T, Inc.; Adobe Systems; Amazon Web Services; Audio-Technica U.S., Inc.; Blackmagic Design; Comrex; Dolby Laboratories; Grass Valley; Ikegami; Limelight Networks; Nautel; Panasonic; Planar; Rohde & Schwarz and Pixel Power; Ross Video; Sony Electronics, Inc.; Telestream; Verizon Business and Verizon Media; Vizrt; Wheatstone Corporation; and WideOrbit, among others.
